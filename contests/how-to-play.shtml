How to Play

Gallop currently offers two separate contests. One is the "Road to the Roses", or RTTR, and the other is the "Road to the Breeders' Cup", or RTTBC.

RTTR runs from January into May, and RTTBC runs from June into November. Players can participate in either/or/both of the contests. One contest doesn't have any dependency on the other.

These contests function like traditional fantasy sports (as opposed to daily fantasy sports). The focus here is on a season-long contest, with weekly scoring events. These type of contests tend to work best when you can participate amongst family and friends and compete against each other.

Specific details on each contest can be found below, along with some details that apply to all contests.

Kentucky Derby

The scope of the RTTR contest will be all of the Kentucky Derby Prep Races (America circuit) beginning after the 1st week in January. This amounts to ~25 races.

In mid-January you'll draft 5 horses, 2 trainers, and 2 jockeys whom you think have a good chance at winning the Kentucky Derby.

There will be 7 races from late January thru mid-February where you can earn points.

The 2nd draft of 5 horses will take place in mid-February.

There will be 10 races following that draft up until the end of March.

The draft at the end of March is a variable draft, meaning you can take anywhere from 0-5 horses.

There will be 7 races following that draft leading up to the Derby.

The week of the Derby, you can use any of those 0-5 horse selections that you didn't use at the end of March to complete your stable.

Come Derby Day, you'll end up with 15 horses, 2 trainers and 2 jockeys in your stable.

Horse points are awarded for the Top 4 finishers in each race. Trainer and Jockey points are only awarded to the Winner.

Breeders' Cup (7 Dirt Divisions)

The scope of the RTTBC contest will be all of the Graded Stakes races on Dirt in the United States beginning with the Belmont Stakes Saturday in June. This amounts to ~114 races.

The races are spread out amongst various tracks:

  • Saratoga (22)
  • Belmont (19)
  • Keeneland (14), including the 7 Breeders' Cup races
  • Del Mar (12)
  • Churchill Downs (11)
  • Santa Anita (9)
  • Monmouth (6)
  • Parx (5)
  • 7 tracks with 2 races
  • 2 tracks with 1 race

In early June you'll draft 7 horses, 2 trainers, 2 jockeys, and 2 sires whom you think have a good chance of succeeding in the 7 Breeders' Cup Dirt Divisions, which include:

  • Classic (3YO and Up, longer distance)
  • Distaff (3YO and Up, FEMALE, longer distance)
  • Dirt Mile (3YO and Up, middle distance)
  • Sprint (3YO and Up, short distance)
  • Filly & Mare Sprint (3YO and Up, FEMALE, short distance)
  • Juveline (2 Year-Olds)
  • Juveline Fillies (2 Year-Olds, FEMALE)

There will be 14 races in June where you can earn points.

The 1st Supplemental Draft of 5 horses will take place in late June, early July.

There will be 17 races following that draft.

The 2nd Supplemental Draft of 5 horses will take place in mid-late July.

There will be 18 races following that draft.

The 3rd Supplemental Draft of 5 horses will take place in mid-August.

There will be 14 races following that draft.

The 4th Supplemental Draft of 5 horses will take place in late August, early September. You will also add on 1 trainer, 1 jockey, and 1 sire during this draft.

There will be 20 races following that draft.

The 5th Supplemental Draft of 5 horses will take place in early October.

There will be 21 races following that draft up until the next draft in early November.

The week of the Breeders' Cup, you will add on 3 Horses to complete your stable.

Come Breeders' Cup weekend, you'll end up with 35 horses, 3 trainers, 3 jockeys and 3 sires in your stable.

Horse points are awarded for the Top 3 finishers in each race. Trainer, Jockey and Sire points are only awarded to the Winner.

All Contests

Stables can have the same horses, trainers and jockeys as other player's stables. The opportunity to separate comes with the "Power Horses" you select each week. Those horses will earn you 2x points.

All horses, trainers, jockeys and sires (RTTBC only) in your stable are eligible to score points every week. There is no "starting" or "sitting/benching" of entries in your stable.

As the contest goes on, the races are worth more points. So even if you stumble out of the gate, you can make up a lot of ground down the stretch!
