How to Play

Gallop currently offers two separate contests. One is the Road to the Roses, or "RTTR" the other is the Road to the Breeders' Cup or "RTTBC".

RTTR runs from January into May, and RTTBC runs from June into November. Players can participate in either/or/both of the contests. One contest doesn't have any dependency on the other.

These contests function like traditional fantasy sports (as opposed to daily fantasy sports). The focus here is on a season-long competition with weekly scoring events. These contests work best when you can participate and compete amongst family and friends.

Please find specific details on each contest below.

Kentucky Derby

The scope of the RTTR contest will be all of the Kentucky Derby Prep Races (America circuit) beginning after the 1st week in January, amounting to ~25 races.

In mid-January, you'll draft five horses, two trainers, and two jockeys who you think have a good chance at winning the Kentucky Derby.

Breeders' Cup
(7 Dirt Divisions)

The scope of the RTTBC contest will be all of the Graded Stakes races on Dirt in the United States, starting with the Belmont Stakes Saturday in June and ending before the BC Pre-Entry Deadline in mid-late October, amounting to ~110 races.

The races spread out amongst various tracks (subject to change pending final schedule):

Data below current as of RTTBC '25.

  • Saratoga (30)*
  • Del Mar (18), including the 7 Breeders' Cup races
  • Belmont at Aqueduct (6)*
  • Churchill Downs (11)
  • Santa Anita (7)
  • Keeneland (7)
  • Monmouth (6)
  • Parx (4)

All Contests

Stables can have the same horses, trainers, and jockeys as other players' stables. The opportunity to separate comes with the "Power Picks" you select each week. Those horses will earn you 2x points.

All horses, trainers, jockeys, and sires (RTTBC only) in your stable are eligible to score points every week. There is no "starting" or "sitting/benching" of entries in your stable.

Race "Nominations" are typically published two weeks before a race. Nominations contain a list of horses eligible to enter a race (pending any "late" nominees). Race "Entries" are then published the week of the race, typically no less than three days before the race. Both Nominations and Entries will be made available on the contest page. You may also go directly to Equibase.com to find these.

All additonal drafting periods are planned around the Nominations schedule to ensure that participants have as much advanced access as possible to potential runners in a future race.

As the contest goes on, the races are worth more points. So even if you stumble out of the gate, you can make up a lot of ground down the stretch!
