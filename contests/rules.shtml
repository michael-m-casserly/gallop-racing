Rules

Contest rules can be broken down into 3 Sections:

  1. Applies to All Contests
  2. Applies to RTTBC Only
  3. Applies to RTTR Only

These rules are grouped accordingly below.

All Contests

FOR-001
During all drafting activities, Individual Stables will be private to the Stable Owner and League Commissioner (see Rule #MIS-001 for additional information on this).
FOR-002
If a jockey were to contract Covid-19 during the RTTR/RTTBC contest, and need to miss time, it will be considered the same as if he were injured. There will be no options for replacements.
FOR-003
If a Trainer is suspended, but his horses still run under an Assistant Trainer's name, any winners will NOT be eligible for RTTR/RTTBC Trainer points. Only horses entered in the Trainer's name are eligible for Trainer points.
FOR-004
All Supplemental Draft picks are FINAL once the respective draft period ends. If a horse gets injured, scratched, etc. you cannot swap him out for another horse.

MIS-001
The League Commissioner will also be a participant in the game. In order to avoid any conflicts of interest, he will set his Power Horses each week before he looks at any other player's stables. He will also draft all of his horses before he views anyone else's stables. In addition, he will notify the league members in any case that applies to Rule #PWH-002, where he needs to switch a Power Horse in his stable due to an early scratch on a Sunday race.
MIS-002
If any situation arises during the game, and is not covered by any of the rules mentioned here, it will be raised by the League Commissioner and brought before the League Membership for a vote. Majority will win, Ties will lose.

PWH-001
Power Horses must be selected by a pre-determined deadline on the day of the first race that week (typically Saturday). Your 3 Power Horses will be locked in for the weekend at that point. See exception in Rule #PWH-002.
PWH-002
One exception to Rule #PWH-001: If there is a Sunday race, and one of your Power Horses from that Sunday race becomes an early scratch (by Noon on Sunday, local track time), then you can contact support@gallop-racing.com directly to swap that Power Horse with another entry that day if applicable.
PWH-003
Late scratches (scratches that occur after the Power Horse selection deadline) will not be considered in Power Horse selection. You will not be able to switch any Power Horses after selection window closes on Race Day.
PWH-004
Stable Power Horse Selections will remain private throughout the selection window of the contest each week in order to protect the integrity of the Power Horse selection process. In other words, we do not want to allow players to gain an advantage or disadvantage based on the timing of when they submit their Power Horses for any given week.
PWH-005
The maximum amount of Power Horses you can select in any given week will be 3. The lone exception to this will be Breeders' Cup Weekend, where you can select 7.
PWH-006
Each week there will be an "Auto-Set" function run after all of the Entries are published. This function will automatically select the 3 Power Horses for any stables that have 3 or less Power Horses entered. These stables will not need to take any action.
For stables that have 4 or more horses entered in any given week, they will need to manually select their 3 Power Horses prior to the deadline for that week.
The Power Horses selection window will vary from week to week, depending on when the final entries are published from each track. At a minimum, you would have all day on Friday to select your Power Horses. In most cases, the Power Horse selection deadline will be sometime on Saturday afternoon (depending on the Post Time of the 1st scoring race.)
These weekly deadlines will always be available on the calendar, and will also be shared via email each week.

SCH-001
If any race is cancelled or rescheduled due to Covid (or any other reason, weather, etc.), it will simply fall into the Race Schedule where it lands, and will be added to the list of races for that given week. This should not impact any Supplemental Draft Dates, assuming the race is rescheduled for a Saturday, but if this happens to become an issue then we will vote on a solution.
SCH-002
If a cancelled race ends up not being rescheduled, then RTTR/RTTBC will simply follow the guidance set forth by the Industry. If the Industry chooses a different race for those horses to have the opportunity to earn those missed points in, then we will add that race (and the respective points) to our RTTR/RTTBC Race Schedule.
SCH-003
Race Schedule is tentative (final scheduled dates will apply to the contest).

SCR-001
Your entire Stable is eligible to score points in every race without any action required on your part.
SCR-002
By a pre-determined time each week, you may select 3 of your horses to act as "Power Horses" for that week. If any of these 3 Power Horses score points in a race that weekend, you will earn 2x those points. "Week" is used to determine race groupings, please refer to the Race Schedule Sheet for more details.
SCR-003
If you don't select any Power Horses, then you will only earn the regular amount of points for any winners.
SCR-004
In cases where a Dead Heat (DH) occurs for Win, Place and Show finishes in Graded Stakes, those horses that finish in a DH will be awarded the total applicable points for that Win, Place or Show position. We will follow exactly what the official Kentucky Derby OR Breeders Cup Points System awards to each horse as the base value, and then that would be multiplied if a Power Horse is selected. On Kentucky Derby OR Breeder's Cup Weekend (when RTKD or BC Points no longer apply), in the case of a DH, both horses will be awarded full points for that position, and the next runner would be placed according to that. For example, if there is a 2-horse DH for the Win, then the 2 horses that finish in 1st will be awarded full Win points, there will be no Place points awarded, and the horse that finishes behind the 2 horses in the DH would be awarded Show points. Same logic applies if there are DH for Place and/or Show Positions as well. In the rare event that there are more than 3 horses involved in a DH, then every horse would be awarded full points for their position, and there would be no points awarded for any of the subsequent positions. Same goes for Winning Sire/Trainer/Jockey, each one would be awarded full points for any Win. Lastly, in case of any discrepancy or confusion, we will ultimately follow whatever the Road to the Kentucky Derby OR Breeders' Cup points process awards the horses, since we are following those points systems.
SCR-005
Any "Split-Division" races will be considered 2 different races. In years past, only the Southwest Stakes has done this. I'm not sure when the decision is made to split races, but I'm putting this rule in place to confirm that if any of the listed races end up becoming a split-division race, then we will simply score it as 2 separate races. This will not impact Power Horse selection either, you can still only select 3 per week.
SCR-006
All Horses, Trainers, and Jockeys that are officially entered into a race are eligible to score RTTR/RTTBC points. This contest will not consider any external situations (such as Bob Baffert's entries not being eligible for Derby Points), in RTTR/RTTBC contest scoring. If players choose to draft a horse/trainer/jockey who is currently involved in pending litigation, then they accept the inherent risk of those selections no longer being available at some point during the contest. There will be no subsitutions available once the contest begins.
SCR-007
The "Official" race results will be used for our contest. These are the race day results that are used to determine the FINAL payouts at the track (including the placement of any "For Purse Only" runners). If a horse subsequently becomes disqualified from a race at a later date (due to a drug violation, etc.), this will NOT change the results of our contest.
SCR-008
Coupled Entries will not be treated as such for scoring purposes. You will only earn points for the placing of the actual horse in your stable, and not his coupled entry.
SCR-009
2x "Power Points" are for Horses ONLY. There are no Power Points for Trainers, Jockeys, or Sires.

SET-001
Please fill out all of the highlighted text items (Column B) on your individual "Stable Entry" Google Sheet (link to be provided) and submit it before the deadline listed on the Race Schedule.
SET-002
Each player will select a Winning Time for this year's Kentucky Derby OR Breeder's Cup Classic. This will be used in case of any tiebreaker at the end of the year. The Player with the closest time (to the millisecond) without going over, will be considered the winner. If multiple players select the same winning time, and are involved in a tiebreaker, then they will be both declared winners.

TIM-001
After a pre-determined deadline on Race Day (typically Saturday), your Stable Entry sheet will be locked. It will be unlocked again after a pre-determined time the following week to allow you to select your 3 Power Horses for that coming week.
TIM-002
All references to time are implied Eastern Standard Time (EST), unless stated otherwise.


Contest Specific

RBC-001
Select 7 Horses, 2 Trainers, 2 Jockeys and 2 Sires before the deadline listed on the Race Schedule. This is considered your "Stable". The Race Schedule will consist of the Graded Stakes races on Dirt in the United States. We will be excluding any Turf or Synthetic track races from that list.
RBC-002
There will be 6 Supplemental Draft Periods. Please refer to the Race Schedule Sheet for the exact dates. During this time, you can add 5 horses to your Stable each time, except for Draft #6, where you will only add 3 horses. In Draft #4, you will also add on 1 trainer, 1 jockey, and 1 sire.
RBC-003
The "RTTBC Points" columns in the "Race Schedule" tab will serve as the final source for the Race Schedule and Point Scoring Scheme. It is initially sourced from the link below.
Any subsequent updates to the content on https://www.members.breederscup.com/Points/PointsSystem will NOT be valid for this game, unless determined otherwise by the League Commissioner and agreed upon by League Participants.
RBC-004
All-Weather (or Synthetic) Tracks will NOT count as Dirt Races.
RBC-005
"Off-the-Turf" races will NOT count as Dirt Races. This includes "Main Track Only" entries or MTOs.

RTR-001
Select 5 Horses, 2 Trainers and 2 Jockeys before the deadline listed on the Race Schedule. This is considered your "Stable". The Race Schedule will consist of the Official Kentucky Derby Prep Race "America" list. This includes the UAE Derby (in Dubai), which is classified as an American race. We will be excluding the Europe and Japan races from that list.
RTR-002
There will be 3 Supplemental Draft Periods. Please refer to the Race Schedule Sheet for the exact dates. During this time, you can add up to 5 horses to your Stable. 1st Supp Draft will be 5 horses. For the 2nd Supp Draft you can choose to do this in any order you like, from 0 horses in the 2nd Draft to 5 horses in the 3rd Draft, or 5 horses in the 2nd Draft and 0 horses in the 3rd Draft, or any combination in between.
RTR-003
The "RTTR Points" columns in the "Race Schedule" tab will serve as the final source for the Race Schedule and Point Scoring Scheme. It is initially sourced from this link: https://www.kentuckyderby.com/horses/prep-races.
This will supercede any conflicts that may arise based on Graded Race Point Scales (for example, some races are listed as a Grade 3, but may have different point structure (the Lexington is good example of this in 2021)) Any subsequent updates to the content on https://www.kentuckyderby.com/horses/prep-races will NOT be valid for this game, unless determined otherwise by the League Commissioner and agreed upon by League Participants.
