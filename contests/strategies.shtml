Strategies

Variable Drafting

The final 2 Supplemental Drafts in the RTTR contest will be "variable". Meaning stables can select anywhere between 0 and 5 horses to add to their stable, over 2 separate selection periods.

The first period will be the first week of April. The second period will be the week leading into the Kentucky Derby.

While there are many unique factors for each stable to consider when making their selections, in the end it amounts to a balancing act and weighing the pros and cons.

Here are some high-level pros and cons to each approach:

Drafting a horse the first week of April (with 7 races left):

PRO: The horse has an opportunity to earn points in those 7 races leading into the Derby.

CON: The horse misses the board in that race, earns no points, and doesn't make it into the Derby.

Waiting to draft a horse during Derby week:

PRO: You know for certain that the horse will be entered into the Derby.

CON: You miss out on any opportunity to earn points in those 7 races prior.

RTTR Points in the Kentucky Derby itself are worth a lot more, so there is definitely some thoughtful consideration required by each stable on their best combination!
