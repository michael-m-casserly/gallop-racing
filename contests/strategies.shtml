Strategies


Variable Drafting (RTTR Only)

The final 2 Supplemental Drafts in the RTTR contest will be "variable". Meaning stables can select anywhere between 0 and 5 horses to add to their stable, over 2 separate selection periods.

The first period will be the week of late March / early April. The second period will be the week leading into the Kentucky Derby.

While there are many unique factors for each stable to consider when making their selections, in the end it amounts to a balancing act and weighing the pros and cons.

Here are some high-level pros and cons to each approach:

Drafting a horse in late March / early April (with 7 races left):

PRO: The horse has an opportunity to earn points in those 7 races leading into the Kentucky Derby.

CON: The horse misses the board in that race, earns no points, and doesn't make it into the Kentucky Derby.

Waiting to draft a horse during Kentucky Derby week:

PRO: You know for certain that the horse will be entered into the Kentucky Derby.

CON: You miss out on any opportunity to earn points in those 7 races prior.

RTTR Points in the Kentucky Derby itself are worth a lot more, so there is definitely some thoughtful consideration required by each stable on their best combination. The most common is 3 then 2, or 4 then 1, or all 5 up front. Rarely are 4 or 5 left until Derby Week. But it all depends on each stable, their current position, stable strength, etc. This is where the RTTR Contest gets interesting!

Road to the Breeder's Cup Contest (RTTBC)

The RTTBC contest is a lot longer and encompasses more divisions than the RTTR contest (which is restricted to 3-year-old horses on the dirt). The RTTBC contest covers all 7 dirt divisions leading into the Breeders' Cup. The 7 divisions include:

  • Classic (3YO and Up, longer distance)
  • Distaff (3YO and Up, FEMALE, longer distance)
  • Dirt Mile (3YO and Up, middle distance)
  • Sprint (3YO and Up, short distance)
  • Filly & Mare Sprint (3YO and Up, FEMALE, short distance)
  • Juveline (2 Year-Olds)
  • Juveline Fillies (2 Year-Olds, FEMALE)

Since the RTTBC Contest covers over 100 graded stakes races, it would be impossible to draft a runner for every race.

The most common approach is to draft runners for the Grade 1 races. The logic being, these are the horses most likely to move onto the Breeders' Cup. In addition, if they happen to lose a Grade 1 race, then they are very likely to come back and run in a lesser Grade 2 or Grade 3 race. Whereas if a horse were to lose a Grade 3 race, it would be unlikely to see them move onto the higher level Grade 2 and Grade 1 races.
